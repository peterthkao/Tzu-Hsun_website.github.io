Research

Research Interests

My research centers on ionospheric physics and space instrumentation. I study how geophysical events — solar eclipses, volcanic eruptions, earthquakes, and geomagnetic storms — drive responses in the ionosphere, and I develop the data systems that make those observations possible.

Wave-Driven Particle Precipitation

Investigating how waves in Earth's magnetosphere drive energetic particle precipitation into the atmosphere, with a focus on the underlying physics and its coupling to the ionosphere.

COSMO CubeSat — Data Processing Lead

Leading data processing for COSMO, a 6U CubeSat that measures Earth's magnetic field using a vectorized rubidium scalar magnetometer. Responsibilities include in-orbit calibration, pipeline development, and science data distribution.

Ionospheric Responses to Solar & Geophysical Events

Analyzing in-situ satellite measurements and ground-based data to characterize how the ionosphere responds to solar eclipses, volcanic eruptions (e.g., 2022 Tonga), geomagnetic storms, and earthquakes.

Magnetometer Data & Geomagnetic Research

Interested in magnetometer data analysis for geomagnetic research, including field calibration and the detection of magnetic signatures associated with atmospheric and geophysical phenomena.
