The ionospheric plasma density hole on the 10 May 2024 Mother's Day great magnetic storm
Wu, T. Y., Liu, J. Y., Lin, C. Y., Chao, C. K., Kao, T. H., and Lee, P. H.
Geoscience Letters, 2025 · doi:10.1029/2025GL115956
▸
  • FORMOSAT-5 and FORMOSAT-7 observed upward ion velocity at 720 km and downward ion velocity at 550 km altitude inside the plasma hole over the Atlantic Ocean during local midnight.
  • The electron density in the hole yields a double-peak structure, with the lower peak at ~440 km and the upper peak at ~760 km altitude.
  • The downward and upward ion velocities are likely responsible for creating the double-peak electron density feature observed during this extreme storm (Dst −412 nT).
Uplift of the ionosphere by tropospheric and thermospheric Lamb waves induced by the 2022 Tonga volcanic eruption
Liu, T. C., Liu, J. Y., Lee, P. H., Lin, C. Y. T., Kao, T. H., and Tsai, Y. L.
Geoscience Letters, 2025 · doi:10.1186/s40562-025-00419-0
▸
  • Thermospheric Lamb wavefronts propagated at 539 m/s and tropospheric ones at 338 m/s, uplifting the ionosphere by 86–155 km and 50–110 km, respectively.
  • The ionospheric uplift significantly increased the Rayleigh–Taylor instability growth rate, resulting in the occurrence of ionospheric spread-F layers.
  • The tropospheric Lamb wave was accompanied by distinct near-surface air pressure pulses and sea level fluctuations, while the thermospheric one was not.
Doppler Frequency shifts associated with the 18 September 2022 M6.8 Taitung Earthquake observed by local CW-HF Doppler sounding systems in Taiwan
Liu, J. Y., Kao, T. H., Chen, Y. C., Chum, J., Chang, W. Y., Huang, B. S., Urbář, J., Hattori, K., Zhao, Y. X., and Chang, Y. C.
Journal of Geophysical Research: Space Physics, 2025 · doi:10.1029/2025JA033876
▸
  • The seismo-traveling ionospheric disturbances (STIDs) from the M6.8 Taitung earthquake propagated upward at approximately the speed of sound, with a horizontal speed of ~1.4 km/s for nearby quakes — slower than the 3.2–3.9 km/s observed for distant ones.
  • DFS observations from five sounding frequencies across nine receiving stations confirmed upward propagation with reflection heights of ~160–225 km altitude.
  • In the Taiwan vicinity, the total intensity of earthquakes is essential for triggering STIDs, established through analysis of the top nine earthquakes during 2022–2023.
Magnetic field signatures of tropospheric and thermospheric Lamb modes triggered by the 15 January 2022 Tonga volcanic eruption
Liu, J. Y., Kao, T. H., Liu, T. C., Huang, B. S., Lee, P. H., Sun, Y. Y., Chen, C. H., Hattori, K., Lee, I. T., Su, C. L., Terng, C. T., and Huang, T. S.
Geophysical Research Letters, 2023 · doi:10.1029/2023GL105393
▸
  • Tropospheric (315 m/s) and thermospheric (487 m/s) Lamb waves from the Tonga eruption activated dynamo currents and electric fields, producing coherent magnetic field deflections in all 10 Taiwan magnetometers pointing toward and away from Tonga.
  • Beamforming analysis on 69 INTERMAGNET magnetometers confirmed that both Lamb modes efficiently activated traveling ionospheric disturbances and uplifted the ionosphere.
  • The tropospheric Lamb wave additionally modulated ground-based air pressures and sea levels, while the thermospheric wave did not show such surface signatures.
Investigation of the aberrant record from bus GPS receiver onboard FORMOSAT-7/COSMIC-2 satellite constellation in low Earth orbit
Kao, T. H., Lee, I. T., Fong, C. J., Liu, J. Y., and Chang, M. S.
Advances in Space Research, 2023 · doi:10.1016/j.asr.2023.08.005
▸
  • Over 7,000 "Warm Start Requested" (WSR) events were recorded in 3 years after launch across the six-satellite F7/C2 constellation, classified as a type of single event effect (SEE) in the commercial COTS GPS receivers.
  • A warning area of high SEE probability was statistically defined based on the geomagnetic field strength of ~21,200/20,550 nT, closely corresponding to the South Atlantic Anomaly region.
  • WSR occurrence correlates with both geomagnetic disturbance (Kp index) and solar activity (F10.7 index), confirming a space weather-driven origin.

Ionospheric Responses of the 8 April 2024 and 21 June 2020 Solar Eclipse Using In-Situ Satellite Measurement and the CODE GIM
Kao, T. H. and Liu, J. Y.
AGU 2024 · Washington, DC, USA · Dec. 2024 · Abstract
▸
  • Compared ionospheric responses to two solar eclipses — the 2024 total and 2020 annular — using in-situ satellite data and global TEC maps from CODE GIM.
  • Both eclipses produced measurable electron density depletions along the eclipse path, with the total eclipse showing stronger and more localized effects.
  • Multi-instrument analysis highlights how eclipse geometry and local time control the magnitude of ionospheric responses.
